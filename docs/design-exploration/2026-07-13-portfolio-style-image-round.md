## Shared contract

Preserve the real portfolio projects, distinct laptop/browser/PDF silhouettes, Renaissance portrait, four verified outcome figures, and workflow inventory. Remove neo-brutalist outlines, repeated frames, excessive dots, irregular paragraph spacing, generic card grids, and rainbow colour.

The workflow acceptance gate is literal: five vertical spines remain visible, while one expands to roughly 70% of the stage. A left list, horizontal tabs, or equal columns fail this gate.

## Round one

### A — Hybrid editorial studio

Warm ivory, contemporary sans, selective editorial serif, quiet blue and moss accents. Hypothesis: the existing direction can be softened without changing its core grammar.

### B — Quiet contemporary studio

Sans-forward, cool neutral, minimal route geometry, dark Bias interlude. Hypothesis: a cleaner product-studio language will remove the neo-brutalist reading.

### C — Soft warm editorial gallery

Parchment, mushroom, olive, dusty blue, cultured serif. Hypothesis: more editorial warmth can make the technical material feel human.

Round-one result: none reproduced the reference accordion accurately. They remain useful for palette, type, and rhythm only.

## Round two

### D — Contemporary atelier

Renaissance proportion and light for narrative; precise device objects for work; folio-spine accordion for workflows. Hypothesis: shared composition and material cues can unify portrait and interface artifacts without making them visually identical.

### E — Museum exhibition catalogue

Curatorial captions, object-as-exhibit layouts, neutral gallery light, archive-shutter accordion. Hypothesis: exhibition grammar can make disparate projects feel like one authored practice.

### F — Cinematic field guide

Alternating luminous paper and charcoal chapters, tactile object lighting, literary chapter titles, field-guide accordion. Hypothesis: stronger section contrast can create narrative pacing while preserving clarity.

Round-two result: all three show the intended accordion structure. D is the recommended base; E has the strongest Selected Work curatorial structure; F has the strongest dark/light pacing and portrait chapter.
