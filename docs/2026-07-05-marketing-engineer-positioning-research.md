# Marketing Engineer Portfolio Positioning Research

Date: 2026-07-06  
Site: `portfolio-remote-preview.vercel.app`  
Purpose: sharpen copy, positioning, and next-site priorities against current marketing-engineer job language and strong portfolio patterns.

## Executive Decision

Keep the site framed around **Marketing Engineer**.

The strongest positioning is not "web designer" and not "AI tools person." It is:

> I build the systems that make modern marketing work: websites, content engines, proposal workflows, reporting loops, and AI-enabled processes that turn strategy into shipped assets.

The current site structure is close. The next pass should be a copy and proof pass, not a redesign:

1. Make the homepage immediately explain the hiring value.
2. Map the work to role-fit categories employers recognize.
3. Keep project/system pages sparse, inspectable, and source-safe.
4. Make the workflow pages prove how the work gets made, not just what tools were used.

## What Current Job Descriptions Signal

### 1. Marketing Engineer is a builder role inside marketing

Profound frames the role as a full-stack marketer with builder skills who sees manual process and builds systems around it. Their framing is useful because it connects AI, marketing workload growth, and human judgment without reducing the role to "AI operator."  
Source: https://www.tryprofound.com/marketing-engineer

Portfolio implication:

- The homepage should say that systems are the work.
- The case pages should show repeatable workflows, not only finished websites.
- "Marketing Engineer" should stay the identity line.

### 2. Senior marketing-engineer roles want the whole marketing engine

Recent senior marketing-engineer listings are asking for website ownership, martech stack ownership, marketing data, dashboards, AEO/GEO/SEO, AI tooling, Claude skills/plugins, landing-page velocity, and translation between marketing, RevOps, data, and engineering.  
Sources: https://jobs.lever.co/sambatv/d4b858c7-eb73-447a-a83a-17e84106082b and https://jobs.lever.co/zerotier/ef247b1e-1428-4c64-bfe1-7a3e9a36d5ba

Portfolio implication:

- The site should present Michael as technical capacity for a marketing team, not as a visual designer.
- The work buckets should map to websites, content/AEO, sales enablement, reporting, and AI enablement.
- Case pages need short "what shipped" modules: site, workflow, dashboard, PDF, CMS entry, report, or tool.

### 3. Growth marketing engineer roles want AI-enabled operating systems

Growth roles increasingly ask for campaign signal architecture, attribution, tracking QA, dashboards, API-connected workflows, AI agents, prompt systems, experimentation frameworks, and decision briefs for demand-generation leaders.  
Source: https://jobs.lever.co/restaurant365/1246dcf6-5971-445b-88fb-44b6eca11f7e

Portfolio implication:

- "AI visibility reporting," "content system," and "proposal system" should be framed as GTM infrastructure.
- We should show how inputs become repeatable outputs, but avoid making the homepage a dashboard.
- The reporting work should get a stronger page because it maps directly to marketing-leadership language.

### 4. GTM/Marketing Engineer roles want revenue infrastructure and publishing systems

GTM/Marketing Engineer listings describe lead capture, enrichment, routing, attribution, landing pages, API integrations, automation workflows, AI agents, content drafting, campaign reporting, CMS/data-source publishing systems, and repeatable playbooks.  
Source: https://jobs.lever.co/influur/f037b650-a2f5-4d0f-8d0e-69ae8fc1046e

Portfolio implication:

- The VI local proposal system and deploy-presentation workflow should be promoted as sales enablement systems.
- The content system should emphasize publishing infrastructure, not just blog writing.
- The built-tools page should read like a proof index for internal tools and workflows.

## What Great Portfolio References Signal

### 1. Portfolio sites need work-first clarity

Awwwards describes portfolio sites as content-focused showcases for professionals and agencies, and its current portfolio/developer categories show that motion, WebGL, AI-orchestration concepts, and interaction can work when they stay tied to a clear body of work. The useful takeaway is simple: the portfolio is valuable when it gives the work a clear creative medium, not when it becomes an internal dashboard.  
Source: https://www.awwwards.com/websites/portfolio/

Portfolio implication:

- Keep the homepage restrained.
- Lead with selected work and visible outcomes.
- Keep literal workflow diagrams on deeper pages.

### 2. Strong portfolios show capability and approach without overwhelming

Creative Bloq's review of Sania Saleh's portfolio highlights a homepage that communicates broad offerings in a structured way, then supports it with approach and values. Their broader portfolio examples also emphasize role clarity, rich thumbnails, selective motion, and explaining the creator's contribution.  
Source: https://www.creativebloq.com/portfolios/examples-712368

Portfolio implication:

- Add a short "What I build" section that maps to four buckets.
- Add "My role" and "What shipped" to project/system pages.
- Keep case pages image-led with concise contribution/proof blocks.

### 3. Portfolio examples are increasingly curated and specific

SiteBuilderReport's 2026 portfolio collection reinforces that portfolio pages need curation, not exhaustive proof dumping.  
Source: https://www.sitebuilderreport.com/inspiration/portfolio-websites

Portfolio implication:

- We should prune public routes and keep only the pages that help applications.
- Keep public proof selective, with deeper evidence available in source-safe pages.

## Recommended Positioning

### Primary positioning line

**Marketing engineer building the systems behind modern growth.**

Supporting copy:

I combine positioning, AI workflows, web development, APIs, and measurement to ship marketing assets that work in the real world: websites, content systems, proposal tools, reporting loops, and sales enablement workflows.

### More direct variant

**I build marketing systems that ship.**

Supporting copy:

I turn strategy, content, AI tools, and APIs into working websites, proposal workflows, dashboards, and repeatable processes for SaaS, ecommerce, service businesses, advertising, and community organizations.

### More job-market variant

**Marketing engineer for AI-enabled growth systems.**

Supporting copy:

I build the technical layer behind marketing: landing pages, content pipelines, API-driven workflows, campaign infrastructure, reporting dashboards, and human-reviewed AI systems that help teams move faster without losing taste or quality.

### Avoid

- "AI web designer"
- "Freelance web designer"
- "Automation expert"
- "Full-stack marketer" as the headline
- "Agency OS" as public-facing language
- Dense "workflow philosophy" copy on the homepage

### Proof Strip

Use only source-safe proof. Suggested pattern:

- Local SEO site with ranked keyword proof
- AI visibility and content reporting across ecommerce accounts
- Proposal workflow with live generated PDF output
- Community website with editorial and operations workflows

Avoid unsourced headline metrics on the homepage. Put draft/claimed metrics in internal notes until evidence is attached.

### Work Buckets

Use four buckets:

1. **Websites and positioning**
   - Cool Runnings
   - RCCV
   - Okanagan Treehouse
   - Vertical Impression public positioning

2. **Content and AI visibility**
   - Search-led content engine
   - GSC/DataForSEO/Firecrawl/Perplexity workflow
   - AI visibility reporting

3. **Sales enablement systems**
   - VI local proposal system
   - Deploy-presentation workflow
   - Custom proposal PDFs, maps, and protected links

4. **Marketing infrastructure**
   - API workflows
   - Google Drive/CMS handoff
   - reporting dashboards
   - skills, MCPs, training, and playbooks

## Page-Level Copy Rules

Each system/project page should answer:

1. What problem did this solve?
2. What did I build?
3. What can the visitor inspect?
4. What proof is available?
5. What remains private?

Recommended section pattern:

- Hero: one sentence of the system's value.
- Visual proof: screenshot, generated output, PDF, or process plate.
- Build path: 4-6 steps.
- Real artifacts: filenames, tools, APIs, live links.
- Output/proof: public links and source-safe results.
- Private-data note where needed.

## Recommended Next Site Moves

### Priority 1: Rewrite the homepage copy

Keep the design. Update headline support copy, work bucket labels, proof strip, and systems intro so the homepage matches the job language.

### Priority 2: Add a "Role Fit" band

A compact section that says:

- I build landing pages and sites.
- I build content and AEO systems.
- I build API-driven marketing workflows.
- I build reporting and enablement systems.

This makes the portfolio easier for hiring managers to map against job descriptions.

### Priority 3: Strengthen project pages

Add a repeated case-page module:

- Role
- Output
- Tools
- Proof
- What is private

### Priority 4: Attach proof sources

Before using any aggressive metric, attach the source:

- Cool Runnings ranking snapshot
- AI visibility reports
- GA/GSC reporting where safe
- VI generated PDF
- dashboard/report screenshots

### Priority 5: Keep deployment clean

Keep using `portfolio-remote-preview` as the public deploy folder. Do not deploy experimental pages like `three-marketing-map.html`, `hero-workflow-map-demo.html`, or `design-wireframes.html`.
