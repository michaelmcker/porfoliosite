# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Hiring managers, founders, marketing leaders, and collaborators reviewing Michael McKerracher as a marketing engineer. They need a fast read on public work, credible results, and the AI-enabled workflows behind the work without feeling like they are inside an internal dashboard.

## Product Purpose

This portfolio proves that Michael can turn research, positioning, APIs, AI tooling, and human review into useful marketing outputs: websites, sales materials, reporting systems, and repeatable workflows. Success means a visitor understands the role fit quickly, sees real public examples, and can click into sparse proof pages for deeper evidence.

## Brand Personality

Precise, warm, and systems-minded. The site should feel like an editorial proof object with technical depth, not a generic designer portfolio or SaaS landing page.

## Anti-references

Avoid dense dashboards, repeated eyebrow labels, generic AI diagrams, neon sci-fi interfaces, heavy linear/mono button styling, fake logos, and work-history details that expose company names outside allowed resume context. Workflow visuals belong on deeper pages; the homepage should stay portfolio-first.

## Design Principles

- Show real artifacts before explaining internal process.
- Keep proof source-safe and anonymized when needed.
- Let imagery carry the work; avoid text-only workflow pages.
- Use AI workflow visuals to clarify how things are made, not to decorate.
- Keep pages sparse enough that the work feels like a showcase, not a dashboard.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Use semantic HTML, readable contrast, responsive layouts, descriptive alt text, keyboard-accessible links, and reduced-motion fallbacks for animation. Dense diagrams need HTML labels or nearby text so the meaning is not trapped inside an image.
